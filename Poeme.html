Frenchman's cards

Hearts of Croissant

I cannot describe,
The joy I feel,
The exitement I show,
At the sight of a croissant.

The croissant's legacy,
Ever present; enduring,
Shall ever be in my heart,
In a place deep down.

Frenchman's best friend,
Ever present; loyal,
I shall never forget it,
Comme hell or high water.



Spades of Baguette

The baguette is a thing,
That is to be reckoned with,
Ever it haunts their dreams,
Like an assassin in the dark.

It is not just a thing,
But a beast, looming; stalking,
But do not fear in our protection,
The protection of the croissant.

But if you take one step,
Against the holyness of our god,
Beware, for the spades of the baguette,
Shall ever strike with peril.



Diamonds of Wine

Diamonds may be dug up,
And wine may be fermented,
But the blood of the croissant,
Is not freely given.

Diamonds may make rich,
And wine may make drunk,
But the blood of the croissant,
Will make you rich in drunkeness.

These diamonds of wine,
Require trial and sacrifice,
But remember: drink,
And you shall thirst no more.



Clubs of Beret

The order of croissant,
Is forever protected,
By the many berets,
And the men underneath them.

Always vigilant; never absent,
Always wary; never weary,
They shall protect our order,
Through strife and destruction.

Blessed with the blood of croissant,
And gifted with the baguette's rath,
Their clubs sitting patiently,
They shall prevail and avail.
